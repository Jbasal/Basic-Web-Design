The History of Babrie

The Birth of an Icon

Barbie’s full name is Barbara Millicent Roberts, named after Handler’s daughter. She made her debut wearing a black-and-white striped swimsuit with ponytail-styled blonde or brunette hair. Unlike traditional baby dolls, Barbie was designed to represent an independent, career-driven woman, which was revolutionary at the time.

Evolution Over the Decades

1960s-1970s: Barbie embraced the changing times with diverse careers, from astronaut to surgeon. 1980s-1990s: Barbie expanded her fashion empire, introduced new friends (like Teresa and Midge), and took on high-powered roles like CEO and presidential candidate. 2000s-Present: Mattel diversified Barbie’s looks, introducing different body types, skin tones, and abilities to promote inclusivity.

Barbie Today

Barbie remains a global cultural icon, inspiring young minds with her message of “You Can Be Anything.” In 2023, she made her live-action movie debut, starring Margot Robbie, further cementing her status as a pop culture phenomenon.
